# Functional Specification

## Background 🌃

Small businesses in New York City face significant uncertainty and risk of failure due to factors such as neighborhood conditions, surrounding business ecosystems, and local infrastructure issues.

Environmental signals such as sanitation complaints, noise issues, and infrastructure problems may influence the economic viability of nearby businesses.

However, these signals are often stored across separate public datasets and are difficult to analyze collectively. Entrepreneurs, planners, and researchers lack a unified tool that connects neighborhood signals with business survival outcomes.

This system addresses the problem by integrating datasets from **NYC Open Data** to analyze and visualize how environmental signals correlate with business survival.

In our Streamlit application, we combine statistical modeling pipelines and an interactive map to allow users to estimate survival risk and explore businesses spatially.

# Data Sources

## NYC Issued Business Licenses

**Dataset Link**
[https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Business/Issued-Licenses/w7w3-xahh/about_data](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Business/Issued-Licenses/w7w3-xahh/about_data)

### Structure

Tabular dataset containing information about licensed businesses in New York City.

### Key Fields

* Business identifiers
* Business category
* License status
* Issuance and expiration dates
* Borough and geographic coordinates
* Address and contact information

This dataset provides the foundation for identifying business activity, location, and operational status.

---

## NYC 311 Service Requests (2020–Present)

**Dataset Link**
[https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Social-Services/311-Service-Requests-from-2020-to-Present/erm2-nwe9/about_data](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/Social-Services/311-Service-Requests-from-2020-to-Present/erm2-nwe9/about_data)

### Structure

Large tabular dataset containing records of public service complaints.

### Key Fields

* Complaint type and descriptor
* Status and resolution information
* Timestamp of request
* Responsible agency
* Geographic location (latitude/longitude, borough, ZIP)

This dataset captures environmental and neighborhood conditions that may influence commercial activity.

---

## Data Integration

The system combines these datasets by linking businesses with nearby service request signals.

Because both datasets include **geographic coordinates**, they can be spatially joined to associate each business with environmental indicators in its surrounding area.

The combined dataset supports:

* Feature engineering
* Statistical modeling
* Visualization

# Use Cases 🔧

## Use Case 1 — Explore Businesses on the Map

**Objective**

User wants to explore the distribution of businesses across New York City.

**Interaction Flow**

1. User opens the dashboard
2. System loads the map centered on New York City
3. Business locations appear as markers
4. User changes dashboard filters
5. System updates visible businesses dynamically

---

## Use Case 2 — Inspect a Business on the Map

**Objective**

User wants to view information about a specific business displayed on the map.

**Interaction Flow**

1. User clicks on a business marker
2. System displays a popup containing:

   * Business name
   * Category
   * Borough
   * Additional metadata
3. User continues exploring nearby businesses

---

## Use Case 3 — Explore Model Insights

**Objective**

User wants to understand environmental signals that may influence business survival.

**Interaction Flow**

1. User reviews outputs produced by logistic regression or cox modeling
2. System presents summary results showing relationships between environmental features and business survival
3. User interprets trends and patterns across neighborhoods

---

## Use Case 4 — Evaluate a Hypothetical Business Location

**Objective**

An entrepreneur wants to evaluate whether opening a business in a specific location may be viable based on neighborhood signals.

**Interaction Flow**

1. User navigates to the hypothetical business simulator.
2. User specifies business information such as:

   * Business category
   * Complaint type and count
   * Number of businesses to compare
3. System applies the modeling pipeline.
4. System displays a summary showing:

   * Graphical outputs
   * Numerical outputs
